01 / Getting started

First-timer essentials.

Genuinely easier than mascara. The first time takes about 5 minutes — after that, 30 seconds. The magnets do the alignment work; you just hold the band near your lash line and let it find its place. Watch our how-to-apply video if you'd like to see it in motion first.

Strip lashes use glue, which dries unevenly, irritates sensitive eyes, and damages natural lashes when peeled off. Santai uses a magnetic liner + magnetic band system — the band clicks onto the dried liner. No tugging, no residue, no commitment. The lashes are also reusable up to 30 times, where strip lashes are typically single-use. See our honest comparison for the full breakdown.

No. Santai lashes are magnet-only — no glue, no pulling, no tugging on your real lashes. The magnetic liner sits on your lash line (like eyeliner), and the lash band attaches to the dried liner — not to your lashes. Removal is a clean lift. Your natural lashes stay yours.

Yes. Our lashes are made from hypoallergenic synthetic mink-effect fibre, and the magnetic liner is dermatologically tested and free from latex, formaldehyde, and harsh adhesives. Many of our customers wear contact lenses or have previously reacted to glue-on strip lashes — and report no irritation with Santai. Most likely: you placed the band too low. Lift, raise 1mm closer to the lash line, and the magnets will re-seat themselves.
Alternative: the magnetic liner may not have fully dried. Wait 30 seconds after applying liner before placing the lash.

04 / Care & maintenance

Making them last.

Up to 30 wears per pair when cared for properly. With our magnet-safe cleanser and flat storage, customers report 3–12 months of regular wear depending on frequency. Daily wearers typically replace every 3–4 months; occasion wearers stretch to 12+ months per pair.

After each wear, wipe the band gently with the included care brush — no water, no soap, no makeup remover near the magnets. If you've worn mascara that day, use our Pure Ritual magnet-safe lash cleanser (available as an accessory). Store the lashes flat in their original compact between wears.

Technically yes — they won't fall off and they're hypoallergenic. But we don't recommend it. Sleeping bends the band against your pillow, which can warp the curve over time. For longest lash life, take them off at the end of the night.

Replace when: the band starts to bow or lose its curve, the fibres clump or thin visibly, or the magnets feel weaker. Most pairs hit 30 wears before any of this happens — but if you're rough on them, it might come sooner.
