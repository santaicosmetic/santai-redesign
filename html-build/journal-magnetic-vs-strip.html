Magnetic vs strip lashes: the honest comparison.

A side-by-side on application time, comfort, lash damage, and real cost-per-wear. No spin — just the numbers we'd give a friend over coffee.

We get this question every single week, usually from someone holding a half-used pot of lash glue. Are magnetic lashes actually better? Or is it just a fad? The short answer is: for almost everyone, magnetic wins on every measurable variable. The longer answer needs five minutes of your morning. Here goes.

1. Application time

Strip lashes done well take 10 to 15 minutes per eye, if you've practised. The glue needs to go tacky (not wet), the band needs to be measured and trimmed, the placement has to be exact on the first try because the second try means a smear. Most people end up at 20 minutes total before they're satisfied.

Magnetic lashes done well take 30 to 45 seconds per eye. There's no glue to wait on. The magnetic liner is already dry. You hold the band a hair above your lash line and the magnets pull it home. If you put it on crooked, you lift and reposition — no smear, no penalty.

For a year of daily wear, that's roughly 90 hours back. Almost four whole days.

2. Comfort during wear

Glued strip lashes feel like glued strip lashes. The cyanoacrylate dries into a rigid resin band; you can feel it tightening as the day goes on, especially at the outer corner. By hour eight you're usually aware they're there.

Magnetic bands are flexible. The magnets are micro-thin (under 0.3mm in the current Santai bands), and the band itself conforms to the natural curve of your lid. We've worn the same pair for 16-hour stretches without thinking about them after the first half-hour.

3. Lash damage

This is the one nobody likes to talk about. Glue removal removes lashes. Even the gentlest peel takes some of your natural lashes with it — usually 2 to 5 lashes per eye per wear, almost all at the outer corner where the bond is strongest. Six months of daily wear and most people notice visible thinning. The "lash strengthening" claims on premium glue brands are marketing, not chemistry.

Magnetic bands don't touch your follicles. They sit on top of your natural lashes, held there by the magnetic liner, and lift off without resistance. We have customers who've worn Santai daily for 18 months and their natural lashes are exactly as they were on day one.

5. When strip lashes still make sense

To be fair to glued lashes — there are still cases where they win:

  • Very specific FX looks. Period drama, theatrical, or competition makeup sometimes calls for a band shape no magnetic brand sells yet.
  • Extreme single-use volume. If you want lashes that look like a small bird is roosting on your eye, the strip-lash category has more options at the dramatic end.
  • Budget at one purchase. If RM 119 in one go isn't possible right now, a RM 5 strip pair is the lower entry point.

That's it. For everyday wear, work, weddings, dates, and almost every other occasion most of us actually have, magnetic wins.

The bottom line

Magnetic lashes are quietly the better answer for 95% of the people still using glue. The category just hasn't been marketed well in Malaysia, which is part of why we built Santai — to make the case in clear language, with bands actually designed for the eye shapes around here.
